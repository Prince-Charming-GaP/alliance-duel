# State Ruler - Defender Strategies

**[← Back to General Introduction](00_general_introduction.md)** | **[← Invader Strategies](03_invader_strategies.md)**

---

The Defender role results from lower Preparation Phase scoring but offers compensating advantages. Home field positioning, reinforcement speed bonuses, and default capital ownership create opportunities for coordinated defensive victory.

Success requires leveraging positional advantages, executing counter-attacks, and maintaining defensive discipline throughout the four-hour battle.

## Defender Advantages

### Home Field Compensation

**Despite Teleportation Restrictions, Defenders Gain:**

**Home Field Advantage:**
- Fighting within established territory
- Familiar positioning and terrain knowledge
- Pre-established defensive structures
- Known ally locations and support distances

**No Teleportation Costs:**
- Already positioned defensively
- No diamond expenditure needed
- Forces naturally distributed around capital
- Immediate defensive readiness

**300% Reinforcement March Speed Boost:**
- When alliance controls at least one city/territory
- 3x faster deployment to crisis points
- Rapid response to threats
- Superior tactical mobility within own state

**Default Capital Ownership:**
- Invaders must reach 100% occupation to win instantly
- Defenders start with capital control
- Percentage advantage from beginning
- Victory possible through preservation strategy

**Home Infrastructure Accessibility:**
- Hospital and healing facilities readily available
- Resource gathering unimpaired
- Alliance structures provide support bonuses
- Familiar battlefield conditions

**TVT Combat Point Generation:**
- Maximum points in capital areas
- Rewards whether winning or losing individual engagements
- Aggressive capital defense economically viable
- Strategic sacrifice generates personal points

### Defender Disadvantages

**Significant Limitations:**

**Cannot Teleport to Enemy States:**
- Fighting only within own territory
- Cannot strike enemy home capital
- No offensive mobility options
- Purely reactive positioning

**Reactive Position:**
- Respond to invader movements
- Cannot choose engagement timing
- Forced to defend rather than attack
- Invaders dictate battle tempo

**Preparation Phase Deficit:**
- Became defender by scoring fewer points
- Suggests organizational or participation gaps
- May indicate coordination challenges
- Requires correction for future rounds

**Sustained Defense Resource Costs:**
- Healing resource expenditure (food, wood, metal, gas)
- Four-hour continuous defense
- High attrition rates if turrets lost
- Deeper resource stockpiles needed

**Coordination Dependency:**
- Defensive success relies on unified response
- Timing critical vs invader concentration
- Communication breakdowns more costly
- Requires tight coordination to counter mobility advantage

## Capital Defense Formations

### Layered Defensive Structure

**Concentric Defense Zones:**

**Capital Building (Center):**
- Maximum 10 troop garrisons
- Highest priority protection
- Strongest defenders positioned here
- Continuous reinforcement required

**Four Turret Positions (Critical):**
- Surround capital building
- Critical defensive structures
- Each accepts 10 troop garrisons
- Prevention priority: enemy control = 2% capital damage per cycle

**Capital Area Perimeter:**
- High-value combat zone
- Elevated point multipliers
- Secondary defensive line
- Intercept invaders before capital

**Level 6 Zones (Secondary Positions):**
- Enhanced point bonuses
- Fallback positions
- Rally staging areas
- Support and reinforcement staging

**Multiple Engagement Zones:**
- Create depth in defense
- Force invaders through multiple layers
- Buy time for reinforcement rotation
- Prevent direct capital rush

### Formation Priorities

**Troop Deployment Strategy:**

**Formation 1-3 (Strongest Heroes):**
- Best hero-troop combinations
- Primary defensive formations
- Positioned at capital and critical turrets
- Optimized stat synergies

**Formation 4 (Premium Pass Holders):**
- Fourth defensive layer if available
- Additional garrison capacity
- Enhanced defensive depth
- Tactical flexibility

**Troop Composition Match Hero Bonuses:**

- **Blood Rose heroes:** Assaulter troops (T9+)
- **Wings of Dawn heroes:** Shooter troops (T9+)
- **Guard of Order heroes:** Rider troops (T9+)

Mismatched factions lose bonuses - coordination essential for optimal stats.

### Defensive Positioning Strategy

**Circular Defense Patterns:**

**Concentric Rings Around Capital:**
- Strongest players closest to capital
- Mid-power players on turrets
- Outer ring intercepts approaching invaders
- Layered resistance delays invader advance

**Obstacle Removal:**
- Eliminate sapphire and cactus obstacles
- Clear attack vectors toward capital
- Force longer approach paths
- Control engagement zones

**Alliance Structure Positioning:**
- Maximize path length invaders must traverse
- Position buildings creating defensive maze
- Utilize terrain for tactical advantage
- Coordinate structure placement

**Garrison Limits:**
- 20-player garrison limit per structure
- 10 troops each from 20 commanders maximum
- 200 troops total per capital structure/turret
- Coordinate rotation for offensive participation

### Hero Selection for Defense

**Top-Tier Defenders:**

**Katrina and Laura (Exceptional):**
- High defense stats
- Crowd control abilities
- Tank capabilities
- Priority defensive heroes

**Sophia and Leah (Secondary):**
- AoE damage output
- Tankiness for sustained combat
- Defensive skill synergies
- Reliable defensive choices

**Passive Buff Heroes:**
- "Regardless of deployment" buffs function even when benched
- Provide stat bonuses without garrison slot
- Strategic bench positioning
- Maximize effective combat power

**Hero Rotation:**
- Keep multiple hero sets ready
- Rotate when primary heroes wounded
- Maintain continuous defensive capability
- Never gap in defensive hero coverage

## Counter-Attack Opportunities

### Offensive Operations from Defensive Position

**Despite Mobility Restrictions, Counter-Attacks Viable:**

**Target Lower-Level Invader HQs:**
- Easy points with minimal risk
- Invaders must defend their own capital while attacking
- Lower-level invader bases (HQ 15-25) vulnerable
- Methodical farming generates substantial points

**Strike Moving Troops:**
- Invaders transitioning between positions
- Vulnerable during redeployment
- Intercept marches in transit
- Timing critical for success

**Attack Resource Gatherers:**
- The moment invaders begin gathering
- Vulnerable while troops deployed
- Easy targets with guaranteed results
- Prevents invader resource acquisition

**Exploit Split Forces:**
- When enemies divide attention between objectives
- Concentrated counter-strikes on isolated positions
- Local numerical superiority
- Overwhelm dispersed invaders

**Hit During Rotation Windows:**
- Enemy reinforcement and troop rotation timing
- Transitioning forces vulnerable
- Timing intelligence critical
- Coordinate with scouts

### Rally Coordination for Counter-Strikes

**Five-Minute Duration Rallies:**

**Concentrated Strikes:**
- Rally coordination enables focused force
- Overwhelm isolated enemy positions
- Time rallies during invader transitions
- Maximum damage in single strike

**Target Selection:**
- Isolated invader marches
- Weakened forces after capital attacks
- Enemy HQs within defender territory
- Resource gatherers

### TVT Focus in Capital Area

**Maximum Defensive Point Generation:**

**Aggressive Capital Defense:**
- TVT combat generates maximum points
- Rewards whether attacks succeed or fail
- Both sides earn points in capital/turret zones
- Strategic sacrifice economically viable

**Rotation Through Combat Zones:**
- Continuous troop presence
- Maximize point generation
- Accept casualties for point earnings
- Maintain pressure on invaders

**Strategic Sacrifice Concept:**
- Even troop losses contribute to personal score
- Capital/turret zone combat worth casualties
- Aggressive defense over passive positioning
- Balance preservation vs point generation

### Target Priority Sequencing

**Efficient Counter-Attack Targeting:**

**Priority 1: Lower-Level Enemy HQs**
- Minimal risk, high points
- HQ 15-25 ideal targets
- 360,000-520,000 points per full destruction
- Methodical farming

**Priority 2: Isolated Enemy Marches**
- Caught between positions
- Transitioning forces
- Reduced defensive support
- Quick strike opportunities

**Priority 3: Enemy Resource Gatherers**
- Gathering in defender territory
- Troops deployed away from HQ
- Easy targets
- Prevent invader resourcing

**Priority 4: Weakened Rally Forces**
- After capital attacks
- Casualties reduce effectiveness
- Strike before healing rotation
- Follow-up on defensive victories

**Emergency Positioning:**
- Keep March Recalls available
- Exit unfavorable engagements
- Reposition for better angles
- Avoid overwhelming enemy forces

## Resource Management for Sustained Defense

### Pre-Event Stockpiling

**Critical Defender Preparation:**

**Food, Wood, Metal, Gas:**
- Maximum storage capacity
- Millions needed for four-hour defense
- Troop healing primary consumption
- Cannot gather effectively during battle

**Healing Items:**
- Hospital supplies critical
- Healing speedups for emergency
- Instant heal items for crisis moments
- Continuous healing throughout battle

**Speedups Saved:**
- Critical moment deployment only
- Emergency troop healing
- Rapid reinforcement
- Don't waste on non-essential uses

**Peace Shields:**
- Offline period protection
- Coordinate shield timing
- Emergency HQ defense
- War Frenzy cooldown consideration

### Healing Cost Management During Event

**Tier-Based Economics:**

T9 troops cost significantly less than T11 to heal:
- Economic incentives for appropriate tier fielding
- Balance combat effectiveness vs healing costs
- T11 for critical positions
- T9 for general defense

**Batch Healing Strategy:**

**Groups of 50 Troops:**
- Fastest alliance help response
- One-hour healing times
- Alliance members click help = instant heal
- Continuous cycle throughout battle

**Hospital Capacity Management:**

**Typical Capacity: 60,000 Wounded Troops**

Critical threshold monitoring:
- Avoid overflow at all costs
- 30% permanent death on overflow
- Strategic withdrawal at 80-90% capacity
- Continuous healing prevents overflow

**Two Talent Point Resets Per Event:**

**War Talents (Combat):**
- Maximum attack/defense bonuses
- Active during engagement
- Combat stat optimization

**Eco Talents (Healing):**
- Accelerated healing speeds
- Active when hospital filling
- Resource production bonuses
- Hospital management critical moment

### Troop Rotation Cycles

**Continuous Operational Cycle:**

```
Deploy → Fight → Hospital → Heal → Redeploy
```

**Implementation:**
- Keep 2-3 formations ready
- One formation heals while others fight
- Never commit all forces simultaneously
- Sustained defensive capability

**Rotation Timing:**
- Monitor formation health
- Rotate before critical casualties
- Maintain continuous capital presence
- Coordinate gaps with alliance

### Gas Management

**Critical Resource for Defense:**

**Gas Requirements:**
- Alliance building attacks: 5 gas required
- Rally attacks: Gas consumption
- Sustained combat operations
- Limited natural generation

**Stockpile Minimums:**
- 200+ gas for event duration
- Pre-event accumulation critical
- Avoid wasteful pre-event consumption
- Emergency reserves for crisis rallies

**Speed-Up Usage Priorities:**

**Save for Critical Moments:**
- Capital defense during peak attacks
- Emergency troop healing during intense battles
- Rapid reinforcement when threatened
- Don't waste on non-essential upgrades during event

## Garrison Mechanics and Reinforcement Bonuses

### Garrison System Details

**Capacity Limits:**
- 20 commanders maximum per capital structure/turret
- Each commander: Up to 10 troops
- Total: 200 troops per structure (20 × 10)

**Garrison Stats:**
- Garrisoned troops use garrison owner's stats
- Strong player positioning crucial
- Hero bonuses apply to garrisoned troops
- Formation synergies important

**Participation Restrictions:**
- Players garrisoning buildings cannot join other rallies
- Cannot garrison multiple buildings simultaneously
- Requires rotation for offensive counter-attacks
- Strategic choice: defense vs offense participation

### Reinforcement System Mechanics

**Alliance Center Building:**
- Upgrades increase Alliance Help Cap
- Increases reinforcement capacity
- Research bonuses apply
- Pre-event upgrade priority

**300% Reinforcement March Speed Boost:**

**Activation Conditions:**
- Alliance controls at least one city/territory
- 3x faster deployment
- Works when:
  - Reinforcing ally HQ in direction of destination
  - Both starting from own HQ
  - Traveling to HQ/city on alliance territory
  - Both HQs inside capital zone

**Does NOT Work When:**
- Troops already deployed elsewhere
- Reinforcing HQ outside alliance territory
- Mixed capital zone positioning
- No alliance territory control

## Defensive Positioning and Zone Protection

### Zone Priority Hierarchy

**Tier 1 Critical (Must Hold):**

**Capital Building:**
- Prevents 100% occupation instant loss
- Maximum garrison at all times
- Strongest defenders positioned
- Continuous reinforcement priority

**Four Turret Zones:**
- Each enemy-held turret: 2% damage per 60-300 seconds
- Maximum 8% with all four held (unsustainable)
- Prevention priority over recapture
- Assign specific defenders per turret

**Tier 2 High Value:**

**Capital Area Perimeter:**
- Maximum point multipliers
- Intercept invaders before capital
- High-value combat zone
- Strategic sacrifice acceptable

**Level 6 Zones:**
- Bonus points for combat
- Secondary defensive positions
- Rally staging areas
- Fallback locations

**Alliance Headquarters:**
- Rally staging base
- Reinforcement coordination
- Strategic value
- Defensive protection

**Tier 3 Strategic:**

**Alliance Towers:**

**Conditional Response Triggers:**
